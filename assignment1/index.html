Endless Summer

Qiuyang Wang S4158360

This project is based on the https://endless-summer.richardmille.com/.

1. First Impression

The first thing that caught my eye was the overall visual backdrop. The scene appears simple yet carefully crafted. On a sandy beach by the sea, amidst the palm trees, stands an aeroplane, with the sun hanging on the horizon. The sky serves as an excellent backdrop, providing a sense of negative space that makes the image feel open and serene. At the same time, this design sparks a desire to explore, encouraging me to continue browsing the interface.

2. Actions During Two Minutes

During the two-minute demo, I followed the on-screen prompts to minimise the browser window and accessed the main interface to read the project overview. I proceeded to the tutorial section, where I followed the instructions to drag the wheel below to advance to the next page. I learnt how to drag the screen to view the entire page, clicked on the ‘…’ to watch the storyboard animation, turned up the volume to listen to the background music, and held down the mouse button to increase the volume and frequency of the ocean waves. I then moved on to the three chapters—Archipelago, Beach and City—and carried out these actions on each page.

3. Most Time Spent

I spent the most time on the ‘Beach’ section. The animations in this part are relatively longer. At the same time, some characters appear repeatedly across different pages, creating a narrative connection. Consequently, I spent more time examining this content.

4. Most Frequent Action

The most common action is to drag the carousel at the bottom of the page to switch between pages. I also tapped the ‘…’ button several times to view the animated content.

5. Main Goal of the Experience

This experience is more about shaping the brand’s image than providing specific product information. It weaves a narrative around the concept of ‘Endless Summer’. The overall design places greater emphasis on emotion, lifestyle and atmosphere.

6. How the Experience Communicates the Goal

This experience conveys the theme of an ‘immersive summer journey’ through visual storytelling, colour shifts and a rhythmic sense of time. Various characters move about, play and socialise within the same setting. The vibrant colour palette and gradient transitions create bold geometric compositions and a sense of playfulness. The twenty-four hours of the day are divided into three chapters, complemented by different perspectives of the same location at the same time.

7. Interaction Over Time

This experience is designed to encourage users to interact with it slowly and steadily, rather than skimming through it quickly. It is better suited to a single, extended session of immersion than to multiple brief interactions. It takes around five minutes to experience the content in its entirety.

8. How Time Interaction is Communicated

This interactive experience unfolds gradually through the rhythm of the animation; with the interface lacking clear instructions or prompts, and no deliberate endpoint, this design creates a sense of continuous flow. However, both the narrative and the gameplay follow a linear path; users need only tap the screen to watch the story unfold—a summer tale set across the region, from sunrise to sunset.

9. Referenced Media Forms

The experience is presented in a cinematic style, with elements such as camera movement and scene transitions. At the same time, its composition and colour palette are reminiscent of fashion or magazine design. Furthermore, certain aspects of the visual style are reminiscent of Memphis design, although this is not explicitly stated on the website.

10. Message About How to Act

These media suggest that users should engage as ‘viewers’ rather than ‘operators’. Interaction involves following the visual narrative rather than active manipulation. The design, reminiscent of films and magazines, allows users to experience the overall atmosphere of the visual communication.

11. Message About How to Feel

The experience conveys a sense of relaxation, luxury and escapism. With just a flick of the finger, visitors can take in the cinematic scenes, whilst the laid-back pace and warm colours reinforce the concept of an ‘eternal summer’, in keeping with the brand’s idealised image.

12. Frustration

There is too much repetition in each chapter; rarely do two pages involve different tasks. Combined with the fact that the interactive experience takes quite a while, this can lead to some people becoming bored during the process.

13. Satisfaction

The most satisfying aspect is the audio. The moment I opened the website, the background music took my breath away; it perfectly evokes a serene, leisurely and sophisticated immersive atmosphere that makes it impossible to tear oneself away from the scene. At the same time, I can hear sounds from various sources, such as cars, skateboards and birdsong. These sounds are clearly layered and even possess a certain sense of spatial depth, with the stereo sound further enriching the experience.
